JEM Tracer

Developed by Stanford students, JEM Tracer automatically traces a cluster of cell branches.

JEM Tracer saves the hours spent tracing by hand and delivers a more accurate trace in a few seconds.

Alpha versions of JEM Tracer are being tested by lab assistants at the Stanford School of Medicine's Yang Lab in the Dept. of Orthopaedic Surgery to research the ability of endothilial cells to aggregate into blood vessel precursors.
