inspiration




Stanford School of Medicine's Yang Lab in the Dept. of Orthopedic Surgery Lab is developing the ability of endothelial cells to form blood vessels in vitro, and as a result these researchers have to spend hours with Photoshop tracing cell culture images which are then processed by a Java library, imageJ, to count nodes, branches, and the general quantitative analysis of the ability of endothelial cells to form tube like structures of vessels. Because this lab has six different project students who trace cells, the tracing of cells are highly differentiated as many of the students have slightly different qualifications for where they should and should not trace a cluster of cells. Therefore, because of this lack of uniformity in the tracing, the results are less consistent and, perhaps, less precise.
